# 30-45 Day Closing Timeline (Realistic)

**Timestamp:** 2025-12-20T20:26:00-05:00  
**Purpose:** Realistic path from second diligence call to close  
**Assumption:** Post-initial alignment, pre-term sheet phase

---

## Timeline Overview

| Week | Phase | Key Activities | Owners |
|------|-------|---------------|---------|
| 0-1 | Alignment | Second diligence call, internal decision | Corp Dev, Legal, Safety |
| 2 | Term Sheet | Draft, negotiate, sign | Corp Dev, You |
| 3 | APA Drafting | Initial APA draft, schedules | Legal (both sides) |
| 4 | APA Negotiation | Redlines, resolution | Legal (both sides) |
| 5-6 | Closing Prep | Board approval, funds flow | Corp Dev, Finance, Legal |

**Total: 30-45 days** (5-6 weeks)

---

## Week 0-1: Alignment Phase

### Day 1: Second Diligence Call

**Participants:**
* Corp Dev Lead
* Legal Counsel
* Safety / Agents Director
* You (Founder)

**Agenda:**
* Integration fit
* Structure preferences
* Remaining concerns
* Next steps decision

**Outcome:**
* Internal decision to proceed (or not)
* Confirm term sheet timing

### Day 2-3: Internal Alignment (OpenAI)

**What OpenAI is doing:**
* Corp Dev briefs Legal and Exec
* Safety/Agents confirms technical fit
* Finance reviews purchase approval process

**What you should do:**
* Send thank-you email (brief)
* Ensure data room is complete
* Wait for their outreach

### Day 4-7: Data Room Review

**What OpenAI is doing:**
* Legal reviews IP documents
* Finance validates structure
* Technical team reviews architecture

**What you should do:**
* Respond promptly to questions
* Provide additional materials if requested
* Do NOT push for timeline updates

**Common requests:**
* "Can you provide X document?"
* "Clarify Y claim in data room"
* "What's the status of Z?"

**Response time:** <24 hours for all requests

---

## Week 2: Term Sheet Phase

### Day 8-10: Term Sheet Drafting

**Who drafts:**
* OpenAI Corp Dev (standard template)
* Reviewed by Legal

**Key terms:**
* Purchase price
* Cash vs synthetic equity split
* Earnout structure (if any)
* Closing conditions
* Timeline

**What you should do:**
* Wait for term sheet to arrive
* Prepare questions (do not send preemptively)
* Review with your counsel (if you have one)

### Day 11-12: Term Sheet Negotiation

**Typical negotiation points:**
1. **Total consideration** ($8M-$10M target)
2. **Cash at close** vs earnout split
3. **Earnout metrics** (if included)
4. **Exclusivity period** (30-45 days standard)
5. **Closing conditions**

**What NOT to negotiate:**
* Transaction structure (asset purchase is standard)
* Indemnification framework (cap, survival, etc.)
* General reps and warranties

**Timeline:**
* First term sheet arrives: Day 8-10
* Your response: 24-48 hours
* Final term sheet: Day 12-14

### Day 13-14: Term Sheet Signing

**What happens:**
* Both parties sign term sheet
* Exclusivity period begins
* APA drafting starts

**What you should do:**
* Sign promptly (within 24 hours)
* Send signed copy back
* Await APA draft

**Pro tip:** Do not negotiate minor term sheet points to death. Term sheet is not binding (except exclusivity). Focus on price and major structure.

---

## Week 3: APA Drafting

### Day 15-17: Initial APA Draft

**Who drafts:**
* OpenAI Legal (lead)
* Your counsel (if engaged)

**Key sections:**
* Purchase and sale of assets
* Purchase price and payment terms
* Representations and warranties
* Indemnification
* Closing conditions
* Disclosure schedules

**What you should do:**
* Review carefully (but don't over-analyze)
* Identify material concerns only
* Prepare questions for Legal call

### Day 18-19: Disclosure Schedules

**What you must provide:**

**Schedule A: Assets**
* Complete IP list (software, docs, domains)
* Trademarks and branding
* Customer lists (if any)
* Contracts being assigned

**Schedule B: Excluded Assets**
* Personal property
* Unrelated IP

**Schedule C: OSS Disclosure**
* Complete SBOM
* License analysis
* Usage summary

**Schedule D: Prior Agreements**
* Confidentiality agreements
* Customer contracts
* Vendor agreements

**Schedule E: IP Assignments**
* Founder IP assignment (executed)
* Any contractor assignments

**Timeline:**
* OpenAI requests schedules: Day 15
* You provide draft schedules: Day 18
* Schedules finalized: Day 21

### Day 20-21: Legal Review Call

**Participants:**
* OpenAI Legal
* Your counsel (if engaged)
* Corp Dev (observer)

**Agenda:**
* Walk through APA draft
* Discuss disclosure schedules
* Identify open issues
* Set redline timeline

**Duration:** 60-90 minutes

**What you should do:**
* Be present (even if counsel leads)
* Answer factual questions directly
* Do not negotiate on the call
* Agree to redline process

---

## Week 4: APA Negotiation

### Day 22-24: Redline Round 1

**What happens:**
* Your counsel (or you) provides redlines
* Focus on material issues only
* Avoid over-lawyering

**Common redline points:**
* Rep qualifications (knowledge vs absolute)
* Indemnification baskets ($25K-$50K)
* Survival periods (12 vs 18 months)
* Earnout measurement mechanics (if applicable)

**What NOT to redline:**
* Standard boilerplate (governing law, notices, etc.)
* Favorable terms (don't negotiate against yourself)
* Seller-protective provisions

### Day 25-26: Redline Resolution

**What happens:**
* OpenAI Legal responds to redlines
* Some accepted, some rejected, some negotiated
* Legal call to resolve remaining issues

**Typical outcome:**
* 80% of redlines accepted or compromised
* 20% require discussion
* Open issues go to principals (Corp Dev + You)

### Day 27-28: Final APA

**What happens:**
* Final APA circulated
* Both parties confirm acceptance
* Signatures scheduled

**What you should do:**
* Confirm acceptance quickly
* Do NOT introduce new issues
* Prepare for closing process

---

## Week 5-6: Closing Prep

### Day 29-32: Board Approval (If Required)

**Threshold:**
* Acquisitions >$10M typically require board approval
* <$10M may be delegated to Exec team

**What happens:**
* Corp Dev prepares board memo
* CFO confirms budget allocation
* Board votes (or delegated approval)

**What you should do:**
* Nothing (you're not involved)
* Stay available for questions
* Be patient

### Day 33-35: Closing Conditions

**Typical conditions:**
* Board approval (if required) ✅
* No material adverse change ✅
* Disclosure schedules complete ✅
* Founder IP assignment executed ✅
* Legal opinions delivered (if required)

**What you should do:**
* Ensure all conditions are satisfied
* Execute any remaining documents
* Provide final disclosure updates

### Day 36-38: Funds Flow Preparation

**What happens:**
* Finance sets up wire transfer
* Bank details exchanged
* Payment instructions confirmed

**What you need to provide:**
* Bank account details (wire instructions)
* Tax forms (W-9)
* Payment allocation (if multiple founders)

### Day 39-42: Closing

**Closing process:**
1. Final APA signed by both parties
2. Disclosure schedules delivered
3. Closing certificates exchanged
4. Funds wired
5. Asset transfer completed

**Timeline:**
* Signatures: Day 39-40
* Funds transfer: Day 40-41 (1-2 business days)
* Confirmation: Day 41-42

### Day 43-45: Post-Closing

**Immediate post-close:**
* Access transfer (GitHub, domains, etc.)
* Integration kickoff (if consulting engaged)
* Announcement (internal only, typically)

**What you should do:**
* Transfer all assets promptly
* Be available for transition questions
* Do not discuss publicly (without approval)

## Critical Path Dependencies

### What Can Delay Timeline

**Week 2 delays:**
* Term sheet negotiation goes >3 days → Add 3-7 days
* Finance approval takes longer → Add 7-10 days

**Week 3-4 delays:**
* Complex APA redlines → Add 5-7 days
* Disclosure schedule issues → Add 3-5 days
* Legal staffing constraints → Add 7-14 days

**Week 5-6 delays:**
* Board approval required but not scheduled → Add 14-30 days
* Bank holiday or payment delay → Add 2-5 days

### What Accelerates Timeline

* Pre-approved terms (see LEGAL_PREAPPROVED_TERMS.md) → Save 7-10 days
* Complete disclosure schedules upfront → Save 5-7 days
* Minimal APA redlines → Save 3-5 days
* No board approval needed (<$10M) → Save 14-30 days

## Pessimistic vs Optimistic Scenarios

### Optimistic (30 days)

**Assumptions:**
* Quick term sheet agreement (3 days)
* Minimal APA redlines (5 days)
* No board approval required
* Clean closing conditions

**Timeline:**
* Week 2: Term sheet signed
* Week 3: APA drafted
* Week 4: APA finalized
* Week 5: Close

**Total:** 30 days

### Realistic (35-40 days)

**Assumptions:**
* Normal term sheet negotiation (5-7 days)
* Standard APA back-and-forth (10 days)
* Board approval or exec review (7-10 days)

**Timeline:**
* Week 2: Term sheet signed
* Week 3-4: APA negotiation
* Week 5: Board/exec approval
* Week 6: Close

**Total:** 35-40 days

### Pessimistic (45-60 days)

**Assumptions:**
* Complex term sheet negotiation (10 days)
* Multiple APA redline rounds (14-21 days)
* Board approval with scheduling delay (21-30 days)

**Timeline:**
* Week 2-3: Term sheet
* Week 4-5: APA negotiation
* Week 6-8: Board approval + closing

**Total:** 45-60 days
