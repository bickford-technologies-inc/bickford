- Integration support for Bickford components into Company's agent runtime

(b) **Knowledge Transfer**
- Training sessions for Company's engineering teams
- Documentation of design decisions and implementation details
- Explanation of OPTR algorithms, promotion gates, and execution equilibrium logic

(c) **Integration Support**
- Assistance with deployment to Company's production environment
- Debugging and troubleshooting during initial rollout

(d) **Strategic Advisory**
- Product roadmap input for Bickford-derived functionality

**No Exclusivity:** Consultant may pursue other professional opportunities provided they do not create conflicts of interest.

## NOTES FOR COUNSEL

**Why Consulting vs. Employment:**
- Avoids employment law complexity (wrongful termination, benefits, etc.)
- Provides flexibility for both parties
- Standard structure for post-acquisition founder retention
- Easier to extend or terminate based on needs

**Key Benefits for Company:**
- Access to founder expertise during critical integration period
- Knowledge transfer reduces execution risk
- Flexibility to adjust engagement based on performance
- No long-term employment commitment

**Key Benefits for Consultant:**
- Professional autonomy
- Ability to pursue other opportunities (within non-compete bounds)
- Clear compensation structure
- Limited liability exposure

**Compensation Strategy:**
- **Fixed Stipend:** Predictable; appropriate if earnout is uncertain
- **Earnout-Linked:** Aligns incentives; consultant benefits from integration success
- **Hybrid:** Balanced approach; base compensation plus upside participation

**Non-Compete Scope:**
- **12 months** is reasonable for narrow scope (directly competitive products only)
- Does not prevent general consulting or unrelated AI work
- Protects Company's investment in Bickford integration
- Enforceable under Delaware law given reasonable limitations

**Integration with APA:**
- Execute concurrently with Asset Purchase Agreement
- Compensation may reference APA earnout structure
- Termination provisions should align with APA post-closing obligations
